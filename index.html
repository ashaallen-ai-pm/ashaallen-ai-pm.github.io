AI-powered weekly scorecard for strategic finance

shipped

How I designed prompt architecture so a VP of Strategic Finance gets the pulse of the business in seconds—and actually trusts it.

prompt design · enterprise AI · finance

The problem

A VP of Strategic Finance needs to open a weekly scorecard and get the pulse of the business—quickly, clearly, no guesswork. Generic AI summaries don't earn executive trust because they lack precision, context, and awareness of what changed since last week.

The AI approach

Designed a four-layer prompt architecture: role (finance analyst persona), context (revenue segments, comparison periods, known risks), task (identify top signals that need attention), and constraints (brevity, precision, uncertainty flagging).

Key product decisions

Scoped AI output to 3 signals max per week—more overwhelms, fewer feels thin. Built explicit uncertainty flagging so the model says "I'm not confident about X" rather than hallucinating. Referenced prior week's scorecard for continuity.

What I learned

The gap between "AI feature that demos well" and "AI feature that earns executive trust" is almost entirely in prompt design. The model was capable from day one—the product decisions around framing the task made the difference.
